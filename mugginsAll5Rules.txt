Start of the game

The first tile is a double, for example:

3/3

This is the spinner.

At first, four directions are available, but the rules you quoted say:

The first and second tiles adjacent to the spinner must be placed against the two sides, then the third and fourth tiles must be placed against the ends.

So play develops like this:

1/3 - 3/3 - 3/6

Only the left and right sides are used first.

Scoring while only one side is occupied

Suppose:

1/3 - 3/3

The spinner still has unused sides, so count:

1 (left end)
3 (top of spinner)
3 (bottom of spinner)

Total:

1 + 3 + 3 = 7

No score.

Once both side positions are occupied
1/3 - 3/3 - 3/6

According to the rules you quoted:

If the first double down has been played on the initial two sides you no longer add the top and bottom sides.

So now count only:

1
6

Total:

1 + 6 = 7

No score.

The unused top and bottom of the spinner do not count anymore.

1 + 6 + 5 = 12

Your example

If 3/3 is the opening spinner, and the left and right sides are already occupied, then by the rules you posted:
