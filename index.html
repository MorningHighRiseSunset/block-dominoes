Block Dominoes

Classic block dominoes with a full double-six set (28 tiles). Match pips on the open ends of the chain — there is no drawing from the boneyard.

Deal

  • 2 players: 7 tiles each (14 dealt; the rest stay unused).
  • 3 players: 7 tiles each (21 dealt).
  • 4 players: 5 tiles each (20 dealt).

This table is you vs CPU (2 players, 7 each).

Setup

  • The player with the highest double leads (or the highest tile if no one has a double).
  • Your hand is face-up in front of you; the CPU’s tiles stay hidden.

Your turn

  • Drag a teal-highlighted tile from your hand at the bottom.
  • Drop it on the gold squares on the board (each domino covers two cells).
  • While dragging, valid cells turn blue with a preview ghost.
  • If you cannot play, you pass.

Winning

  • Go out: play your last tile — you win immediately.
  • Blocked: every player passes in one full round — lowest pip count left in hand wins (in a 2-player game, that means both pass back-to-back).

HUD

The footer shows the open left and right pip values on the chain.

The train grows on the chess grid and turns at the edges — tiles in the chain touch end-to-end.
