Block Dominoes

Classic block dominoes with a full double-six set (28 tiles). Match pips on the open ends of the chain — there is no drawing from the boneyard.

Deal

  • 2 players: 7 tiles each (14 dealt; the rest stay unused).
  • 3 players: 7 tiles each (21 dealt).
  • 4 players: 5 tiles each (20 dealt).

This table is you vs CPU (2 players, 7 each).

Setup (Traditional Rules)

  • The player with the highest double leads (or the highest tile if no one has a double).
  • Your hand is face-up in front of you; the CPU’s tiles stay hidden.

Traditional Domino Rules

  • Big 6/5/4: At game start, you'll be asked if you have double-6, double-5, or double-4.
  • Doubles: Double tiles are placed perpendicular (crosswise) to the chain.
  • First tile: The opening tile is placed vertically.

Your turn

  • Click a teal-highlighted tile from your hand to select it (turns blue when selected).
  • Click a gold marker on the board to place the selected tile.
  • If a tile can play on multiple ends, you choose which end by clicking the corresponding marker.
  • Click elsewhere to deselect a tile.
  • If you cannot play, you pass automatically.

Winning

  • Go out: play your last tile — you win immediately.
  • Blocked: every player passes in one full round — lowest pip count left in hand wins (in a 2-player game, that means both pass back-to-back).

Pip count is the total number of dots on all your tiles. For example, a 5-4 domino has 9 pips (5 + 4), and a double-6 has 12 pips (6 + 6). Lower pip count is better when blocked.

CPU Strategy

The computer opponent tries to:

  • Play its last tile to win immediately
  • Reduce its pip count by playing high-value tiles (like 6-6, 6-5)
  • Block you by leaving ends you can't match
  • Keep options open by saving tiles that match current board ends

HUD

The footer shows the open left and right pip values on the chain.

The chain grows across the table and turns at the edges — tiles touch end-to-end.
